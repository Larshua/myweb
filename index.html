Fable

In literature, a fable (pronounced fey-buhl) is a short fictional story that has a moral or teaches a lesson. Fables use humanized animals, objects, or parts of nature as main characters, and are therefore considered to be a sub-genre of fantasy.

The word fable comes from the Latin "fābula," meaning discourse or story.

STORY DICTIONARY

  • Pack: A group of wolves that live and hunt together.
  • Alpha: The myth that wolf packs are led by an "alpha wolf" originated from studies of captive wolves.
  • Beta: A wolf that ranks second in a wolf pack's social order, below the alpha couple.
  • Omega: The lowest ranking member in the social order of a wolf pack. Natural wolf packs may not have a single omega wolf.
